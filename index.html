    About Me

    Hi, I'm Sakshi Agarwala, a Research Associate specializing in speech processing and document understanding systems. I recently completed my B.Tech in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering from Ramaiah Institute of Technology with a CGPA of 8.6, combining my engineering background with expertise in AI, machine learning, and audio engineering.

    As a Research Associate at SPIRE Lab, IISc, I develop speech segmentation, detection, and transcription pipelines using VAD and Whisper ASR. I automate audio clipping and quantitative analysis for dataset augmentation, build multilingual language detection and analytics with similarity metrics on large audio corpora, and manage end-to-end audio annotation workflows. I also address ASR/transcription quality through region-based review and data analysis.

    Previously, as a Research Intern, I deployed and fine-tuned Kosmos, YOLO, and Surya OCR models for document understanding. I built OCR pipelines using Python, OpenCV, and PyTesseract, evaluated model accuracy using WER and CER metrics via jiwer, and contributed to LLM pipelines with COMET score evaluations.

    My technical expertise spans Machine Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Computer Vision, Speech Processing, and Cloud Computing. I've worked on diverse projects ranging from assistive walker balancing systems using deep RL to movie recommendation systems and IoT-based smart monitoring solutions.

    Beyond technical work, I serve as President of Ecell Ramaiah, where I've led initiatives that increased student engagement by 35% and organized 20+ entrepreneurship events. I've also secured second place in IISc Bengaluru's Urban Vision Hackathon and have research publications in control systems and reinforcement learning.

    Assistive Walker Balancing System

    Timeline: November 2024 - June 2025

    Techstack: Python, PyBullet, SB3, URDF

    Engineered custom URDF models for assistive walker and CartPole systems with realistic physics simulation using PyBullet, implementing differential-drive mechanics, IMU sensors, and joint dynamics for comprehensive robotic control research. Developed and benchmarked algorithms using Stable Baselines3 framework, achieving superior performance with deep RL methods over classical control in continuous action spaces. Created custom Gymnasium-compatible environments with observation spaces including pole angle/velocity, base pose, wheel velocities, and IMU data, enabling systematic evaluation of balance stability and response time metrics. Implemented comparative analysis framework measuring episode length, cumulative reward, and stability metrics, demonstrating algorithms' enhanced sample efficiency and robustness in continuous control tasks.
